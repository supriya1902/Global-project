Endangered Species

Tiger

Adorned with a striped, reddish-orange coat and equipped with an acute sense of hearing and night vision, tigers are one of nature’s most revered and feared predators.

Giant Panda

This peaceful creature, with its distinctive black and white coat, is adored globally. WWF has been active in panda conservation since the 1980s.

Black Rhino

Snow leopards, also known as the ‘ghosts of the mountains’ for their puzzling elusiveness, are among the least known big cats of the world. Sadly, their populations are in decline.

Leopard

Snow leopards, also known as the ‘ghosts of the mountains’ for their puzzling elusiveness, are among the least known big cats of the world. Sadly, their populations are in decline.

Gorillas

Gorillas, the largest living primates, are critically endangered due to habitat destruction, poaching, and disease. Found primarily in the dense forests of Central and West Africa, these intelligent and social animals play a crucial role in maintaining their ecosystems.

Whale

The whale, one of the largest and most majestic marine mammals, is classified as an endangered species due to various human activities and environmental threats. Commercial whaling, habitat destruction, climate change, and ocean pollution have significantly reduced whale populations worldwide.

Dolphin

Dolphins are highly intelligent marine mammals that play a vital role in maintaining the balance of ocean ecosystems. However, several species of dolphins are endangered due to human activities.

Cheetah

With fewer than 7,000 individuals left in the wild, conservation efforts focus on protecting their habitats, reducing human-wildlife conflicts, and promoting breeding programs. As the fastest land animal, the cheetah plays a crucial role in maintaining ecosystem balance, making its conservation essential for biodiversity.
